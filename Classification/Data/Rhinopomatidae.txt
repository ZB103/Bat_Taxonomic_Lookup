*Location
North Africa, South Asia
*Habitat
Arid desert, subtropical/tropical dry shrubland, and semiarid. Roost in caves, cracks in rocks, houses, and Egyptian pyramids
*Diet
Insectivorous
*Social Structure
Live in colonies of thousands of individuals where they gather in small, scattered groups of one or both sexes.
*Reproduction
Breeding season occurs between February and April, with one offspring annually. Not much is known about their reproduction.
*Seasonal Behavior
Aestivation, hibernation
*Lifespan
(Unknown)
*Predators
Bark owls

None

*Genuses
Rhinopoma
*Appearance
Small bats with very slim limbs and a long, hairless, mouse-like tail after which they are named. They have a small snout with a simple noseleaf, big ears connected to eyes by a band of skin across the forehead, and large black eyes. They can be gray, brown, and yellow in color.
