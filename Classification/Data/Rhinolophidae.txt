*Location
Africa, Australia, Asia, Europe, Oceania
*Habitat
Forested and unforested, majority occurring in tropical and subtropical areas. They roost in a variety of places including buildings, caves, tree hollows, and foliage.
*Diet
Insectivorous
*Social Structure
Social structure varies. Some species are solitary while others live in colonies of thousands of individuals. In some species, the sexes segregate annually when females form maternity colonies.
*Reproduction
At least one species is monogamous (Rhinolophus sedulus) and at least one is polygynous as most bats are. They have one offspring at a time. Not much is known about most of the species.
*Seasonal Behavior
Hibernation
*Lifespan
6-7 years; one horseshoe bat lived over 30 years
*Predators
Hawks, eagles, kites, falcons, owls, snakes, domestic cats. Parasites mites, ticks, fleas, and bat flies.
*Threats
Cave disturbances
*Status
Vulnerable (VU) to Least Concern (LC)
Endangered (EN) (13 species)
Critically Endangered (CE) (Rhinolophus hilli)
*Genuses
Rhinolophus
*Appearance
Small to medium bats with reddish-brown, blackish, or bright orange-red full. Their noseleafs are large and horseshoe-shaped, which is where they get their name. 
*Size
35-110 mm
*Echolocation?
True
